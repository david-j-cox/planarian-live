Player not loading? — fixes for "Error #1000" / a blank player

If you see "Error #1000 - the video download was cancelled," the stream itself is fine - it is your browser, not the rig.

Most common fix: make sure you are logged in to Twitch. Open twitch.tv, sign in, then come back and reload this page. The embedded player needs a signed-in Twitch session to start. Still stuck? Try the options below.

Fastest test: is it really my browser?
  1. Open this same page in a different browser (or a private / incognito window, which runs without your extensions).
  2. If the stream plays there, the problem is a setting or extension in your main browser - use the fixes below to fix it there.
  3. If it fails everywhere, the stream may just be briefly offline - try again in a minute or open it on Twitch.
1. Pause your ad blocker / privacy extension
  1. Look at the top-right of your browser toolbar for an extension icon - e.g. uBlock Origin (red shield), AdBlock (stop-hand), Ghostery (ghost), Privacy Badger.
  2. Click that icon, then click its big power / "pause on this site" button.
  3. Brave browser: click the orange lion icon in the address bar and switch Shields to Down for this site.
  4. Reload the page: Cmd+R (Mac) or Ctrl+R (Windows).
2. Allow third-party cookies for Twitch
  1. Chrome / Edge: Settings → Privacy and security → Third-party cookies → under "Allowed to use third-party cookies" click Add and paste [*.]twitch.tv. Reload the page.
  2. Safari: menu bar → Safari → Settings → Privacy → uncheck "Prevent cross-site tracking" (and make sure "Block all cookies" is off). Reload.
  3. Firefox: click the shield icon at the left of the address bar → turn Enhanced Tracking Protection to Off for this site. Reload.
3. Reload the player, or just watch on Twitch
  1. In the black player box, click the red "Click Here to Reload Player" button.
  2. Still stuck? Watch the rig directly here: twitch.tv/behavioraldatasciencelab.

How it works

The video you see above is real-time. The tracking plot takes a different path: 1-minute clips upload to Google Drive, and a second Mac pulls and processes them with a YOLO tracker + behavior model every 90 seconds, then publishes the plot here - so the data trails the video by about 3 minutes.

How well do the models perform?

Location tracking

YOLO detector + temporal cleanup, scored against 200 hand-labeled frames.

For scale, the dish is about 50 mm across — a typical error is roughly 1% of the arena.

Behavior classification

5-class model, leave-one-out cross-validation (F1 per behavior; 1.0 is perfect).

0.84overall (macro-F1)
0.92gliding
0.89contracted
0.86resting
0.86turning
0.65wig-wag

Numbers are from held-out evaluation on this rig's footage. On the live plot, the locomotion states (gliding / resting / turning) are read directly from the worm's motion, while the model supplies the postural calls (contracted, wig-wag).
